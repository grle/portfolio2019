BACKGROUND

I started being interested in learning more about tech when I came to college as a freshman in the engineering program and took a computer science class. While I later switched out to get my degree in Linguistics and Comparative Literature , I graduated with a minor in computer science, and took classes looking into how technology can affect how we look into learning. Before I graduated, I took a class in Digital Humanities, which let me see the possibilities of how using tech to design learning systems can help students and teachers alike in the classroom. This inspired me to get a masterâ€™s degree in Learning Science Technology , where I further learned about the ways in which we can incorporate tech to improve classroom curriculum.

Being a UX designer allows me to combine the interests I have in technology and the humanities, and my hope is that I can use my background in education to influence how tech could be used to create intuitive programs. Beyond my training in graduate school, I have hands on experience using tech in educational places, whether it be in museums or the classroom. I find inspiration from the totality of my experience and interest into my work, and hope that I can only grow as a designer from here.
